My Writings

This writing explores the rise and controversy of Zuowang Yeon, a renowned paleontologist and entrepreneur in Orange County. It highlights his groundbreaking work in fossil research, cloning, and genetic engineering through NexaGenesis, and examines how his commercial success has impacted local communities, ecosystems, and public health.

Living or Clones, Organisms or Fossils

This writing follows the narrator’s eerie journey through an abandoned, decaying mansion hidden beyond forgotten roads. Through vivid sensory imagery and detailed descriptions of the house’s deteriorating grandeur, the piece builds a gothic atmosphere filled with mystery and tension. As the narrator explores the space, they are drawn to a hidden room where an unexpected, haunting revelation awaits them.

Veil of the Familiar

This writing captures a day in the life of a homeless boy through a rhythmic, almost poetic sequence of events. Structured around a counting motif, it illustrates his struggle with hunger, invisibility, and survival. The piece uses sensory imagery and sound cues to emphasize the boy’s resilience and quiet suffering amid the indifference of the world around him.

Ravenousness of the Fiend

This is a descriptive narrative that follows the escape attempt of a caged animal longing for freedom. The piece vividly captures the animal’s emotions, memories of past trauma, and fleeting hope for liberation, only to end with a heartbreaking return to captivity and human cruelty.

A Cage Beyond the Bars

This piece explores the ethical responsibilities engineers face when dealing with defective products, using a fictional scenario to highlight the conflict between legal compliance and consumer safety. It emphasizes the importance of prioritizing public well-being over short-term profit, underscoring how such choices are crucial for maintaining trust and integrity within professional engineering practice.
